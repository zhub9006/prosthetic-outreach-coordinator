# Clinical Trials Reference — Affordable Prosthetic Solutions

This document catalogs key clinical trials relevant to our prosthetic outreach initiative, focusing on affordable prosthetic solutions and cost-effective interventions.

## 🏥 Trial 1: ReMotion Prosthetic Knee (Reverse Innovation)

| Field | Details |
|-------|---------|
| **NCT ID** | NCT04700085 |
| **Sponsor** | Sint Maartenskliniek |
| **Status** | Completed (2019) |
| **Focus** | $80 affordable mechanical prosthetic knee |
| **Conditions** | Lower limb amputation (above-knee) |
| **Enrollment** | 9 participants |
| **Study Type** | Randomized crossover |

### Key Findings
- The **ReMotion Knee** ($80) is a mechanical prosthetic knee developed by D-Rev (San Francisco, CA) for use in low-income countries.
- It has been approved to **ISO 10328** standards and received the **CE Mark**.
- Polycentric design with a lifespan of ~3–5 years (comparable to standard mechanical knees).
- **Does NOT** include pneumatic/hydraulic systems, significantly reducing cost.
- Standard care mechanical knees in the US/Europe cost **$1,000–$5,000** — the ReMotion Knee is a **96–98% cost reduction**.
- Provides basic functional mobility at a fraction of the cost of standard knees.

### Relevance to Outreach
- **Reverse innovation**: Products designed for low-income countries can be adapted for high-income countries to reduce costs.
- The $80 knee could serve as a interim/entry-level prosthetic for underserved populations.
- Demonstrates that affordability doesn't have to mean poor quality — ISO certification ensures safety.
- Ideal for our outreach: can be used to educate communities about affordable options and advocate for insurance coverage.

### Key Outcomes Measured
- L-test (functional mobility)
- Four Square Step Test (precision stepping)
- Berg Balance Scale (static balance)
- Modified Emory Functional Amputation Profile
- Walking comfort, balance trust, fatigue, and performance (VAS scales)

## 🏥 Trial 2: 3D Printing for Mandibular Implant Overdentures

| Field | Details |
|-------|---------|
| **NCT ID** | NCT06155630 |
| **Sponsor** | McGill University |
| **Collaborator** | ITI International Team for Implantology, Switzerland |
| **Status** | Not Yet Recruiting (est. start Nov 2025) |
| **Focus** | CAD/CAM + 3D printing for dental prostheses |
| **Conditions** | Edentulous Mouth/Jaw |
| **Enrollment** | 26 participants |
| **Study Type** | Randomized cross-over |

### Key Findings (Expected)
- **Digital overdentures** (CAD/CAM + 3D printing) can be provided **faster and at lower cost** than traditional methods.
- DLP 3D printing with Dentca Resin reduces fabrication steps and material waste.
- Expected to enhance access to oral healthcare, especially for the elderly.
- Patient-reported outcomes include satisfaction, comfort, stability, aesthetics, and ease of cleaning.
- Cost data will be collected comparing digital vs. traditional workflows.

### Relevance to Outreach
- **3D printing** is a game-changer for affordable prosthetics — same technology can be applied to limb prosthetics.
- Demonstrates the viability of **digital workflows** that reduce cost and turnaround time.
- Our outreach can advocate for 3D printing adoption in prosthetics clinics.
- Connects dental and limb prosthetics: same digital fabrication principles apply.

## 🏥 Trial 3: NMES for Transtibial Amputation (Cost-Effective Rehabilitation)

| Field | Details |
|-------|---------|
| **NCT ID** | NCT06830876 |
| **Sponsor** | O&P Clinical Innovations |
| **Investigator** | Sara Peterson-Snyder |
| **Status** | Recruiting (started Dec 2024) |
| **Focus** | Home-based Neuromuscular Electrical Stimulation |
| **Conditions** | Amputation (transtibial) |
| **Enrollment** | 20 participants |
| **Study Type** | Single-group repeated measures |

### Key Findings (Expected)
- **NMES** is a non-invasive, cost-effective method for rebuilding muscle mass in individuals with transtibial amputation.
- Home-based intervention using a portable device (Chattanooga Continuum) reduces clinic visits and costs.
- 8-week program targets VMO, tibialis anterior, and gastrocnemius muscles.
- Ultrasound imaging validates muscle composition changes.
- Aims to reduce healthcare burdens and improve prosthetic fit/stability.

### Relevance to Outreach
- **Home-based rehabilitation** dramatically reduces cost barriers for amputees.
- NMES can be combined with our outreach: teach patients about affordable rehab options.
- Portable devices are accessible — our outreach can mail or lend them to patients.
- Reduces the need for expensive in-clinic therapy sessions.

## 🎯 Outreach Strategy Recommendations

1. **Advocate for $80 mechanical knees** — use ReMotion data to push insurers and clinics to stock affordable options.
2. **Promote 3D printing adoption** — partner with clinics transitioning to digital workflows for cost savings.
3. **Distribute NMES home kits** — reach underserved amputees who can't afford clinic-based rehab.
4. **Organize regional workshops** — use trial findings to educate communities in Chicago, Boston, and Atlanta.
5. **Build a reverse-innovation case** — show how low-cost solutions from emerging markets can work in the US.
